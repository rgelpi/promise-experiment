## Third-Party Punishment Game

In this experiment, we conduct a variant of the Third-Party Punishment Game (Fehr & Fischbacher, 2004). In this modified Dictator game, a third participant (the "punisher") is able to propose a punishment rate in response to a dictator's proposed division of money between themselves and the receiver. The punisher's proposed punishment is then rated by a community assessment.

### Punisher

1. Participants are introduced to their role as a punisher in the third-party punishment game and asked a series of attention/knowledge questions to ensure comprehension of the task setup and their role.
2. Participants see a dictator's proposed division of money between themselves and the receiver. They are asked to propose a punishment rate in response to this division, which is between 0 and 100%. 
3. Before making a final decision, the punisher is shown a community assessment of their proposed punishment rate. They can revise their punishment rate based on this assessment and provide a final punishment rate.

### Community Assessment

1. Participants are introduced to their role as an observer who is witnessing rounds of interactions between a dictator, receiver, and a punisher in the third-party punishment game. As above, we provide instructions about the task they will complete and ask a series of attention/knowledge questions to ensure comprehension of the task setup and their role.
2. After completing the introduction, participants are provided some examples of divisions between the dictator and receiver, and the punishment rate proposed by the punisher, and why this punishment rate might be considered "fair". 
3. They are then shown 15-25 proposed punishment rates and asked to provide reasons for why this punishment rate is or is not fair and a final evaluation of whether the punishment rate is fair or not. They are told that if their evaluation matches the evaluation of the majority of participants in C, they will receive a bonus payment.
