## Ultimatum/Dictator Game

In this experiment, we conduct a variant of the Ultimatum Game (as well as the closely related Dictator Game). 

Participants are divided into three groups: A, B, and C. Groups A and B take the role of the "proposer" and the "responder", respectively. Group C participants are shown an anonymized interaction between a proposer and a responder and are asked to make judgments about the fairness of the proposer's offer and the responder's decision.

The format for the experiment is as follows:

### Groups A and B

1. Before completing the experiment, participants are introduced to the structure of the experiment (as below). We then ask a series of attention/knowledge questions to ensure comprehension of the task setup and their roles.
2. Two participants from groups A and B will be randomly paired. 
3. The participant from group A (the "proposer") will be asked to divide a sum of money (e.g., $10) between the two participants. The proposer will also include a free-form message of less than 500 characters to the participant from group B (the "responder"). The message can contain anything, but must not contain any information that could be used to identify the proposer.
4. Before A makes a final decision, A will be given feedback from a participant from group C (the community assessment) about whether their proposed division is considered fair. 
5. A is permitted to revise their final proposal. If this proposal differs from the previous offer, it will also receive an updated community assessment.
6. In some versions of the experiment, B may be given access to A's message and community assessment (as well as the revised proposal) before making their decision. In other versions, B will only see the final proposal.
7. In the Ultimatum Game variant, B can accept or reject the offer. If the offer is rejected, both participants will receive nothing. If the offer is accepted, the money will be divided as proposed. In the Dictator Game variant, B has no ability to reject the offer and the money will be divided as proposed regardless of B's preference.

### Group C

Participants in Group C will be shown an anonymized interaction between a proposer and a responder and be asked to make judgments about the fairness of the proposer's offer and the responder's decision.

1. Participants in Group C will be introduced to their role as an observer who is witnessing rounds of interactions between two other participants (A and B) in the ultimatum game/dictator game. As above, we provide instructions about the task they will complete and ask a series of attention/knowledge questions to ensure comprehension of the task setup and their roles.
2. After completing the introduction, participants are provided some examples of messages and introduced to some reasons why messages might or might not be interpreted as 'promises'.
3. They are then shown 15-25 messages from participants from group A and asked to provide reasons for why this message is or is not a promise and a final evaluation of whether the message is a promise or not. They are told that if their evaluation matches the evaluation of the majority of participants in C, they will receive a bonus payment.
