## Trust Game/Investment Game

In this experiment, we conduct a variant of the Trust Game.

Participants are divided into three groups: A, B, and C. Groups A and B take the role of the "trustor" and the "trustee", respectively. A can choose to send money to B, which will then be multiplied by 3. Before making their decision, B can send a free-form message to A about what they will do with the money (which A can see before making their decision). B can choose to not send the money back, or choose to roll a die to determine whether to send the money back (where A receives a positive return on their investment with 5/6 probability and nothing with 1/6 probability).

Group C participants are shown an anonymized interaction between a trustor and a trustee and are asked to make judgments about whether it would be wrong for the trustee to not return the money to the trustor given the message that was sent.

### Groups A and B

1. Before completing the experiment, participants are introduced to the structure of the experiment (as below). We then ask a series of attention/knowledge questions to ensure comprehension of the task setup and their roles.
2. Two participants from groups A and B will be randomly paired.
3. The participant from group A (the "trustor") will receive an initial endowment of $10. They can choose to send this money to a participant from group B (the "trustee"), which will then be multiplied by 3.
4. Before A chooses whether to send the money, B may choose to send a free-form message of less than 500 characters to A.
5. B will receive feedback from the community assessment regarding whether it would be considered "wrong" for B to not return the money to the trustor given the message that was sent.
6. Based on the feedback received, A chooses whether to send the money or not.
6. B can then choose whether to return the money to A. If B chooses to return the money, A receives nothing and B keeps the entire amount. If B chooses not to return the money, A receives a positive return on their investment with 5/6 probability and nothing with 1/6 probability.

### Group C

1. Before completing the experiment, participants are introduced to the structure of the experiment (as below). We then ask a series of attention/knowledge questions to ensure comprehension of the task setup and their roles.
2. Participants in Group C will be shown several examples of messages between trustors and trustees in the trust game.
3. After reading these examples, participants will observe a series of interactions between a trustor and a trustee in the trust game. For each interaction, the participants in group C will be shown the message sent by the trustor to the trustee.
4. They will be asked to evaluate whether they believe it would be considered "wrong" for the trustee to fail to roll the die in light of the message they sent to the trustor. Participants are told that they will be rewarded for matching the evaluation of the majority of participants in Group C.
