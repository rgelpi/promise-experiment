# Occam's razor and the impartial spectator: explaining 'social preferences' as rational action

In this series of experiments, we replicate the several classic behavioural economics experiments, each one with seveal key manipulations.

1. First, some participants ("senders") can send free-form messages to an individual they have been paired with ("receiver"). These messages may include 'promises' about a participant's intent to share payoffs in a fair fashion (e.g., in an ultimatum game or trust game). 
2. Second, when individuals are making choices, we provide individuals with a signal in the form of a community assessment (composed of other study participants' judgments in prior rounds) of a particular choice or offer as 'fair' or 'unfair'. In some variants of the experiment, the sender will be told that the receiver will also see these judgments. 
3. After receiving a community assessment, the sender may revise their initial offer and receive updated community assessments.

The experiments will include variants of the following economic games:
- Ultimatum/Dictator Game (`docs/experiments/ultimatum-game.md`)
- Trust Game (`docs/experiments/trust-game.md`)
- "Power to take" game (`docs/experiments/power-to-take-game.md`)
- Third-party punishment game (`docs/experiments/third-party-punishment-game.md`)

See the respective files for more information on the details of each experiment.
